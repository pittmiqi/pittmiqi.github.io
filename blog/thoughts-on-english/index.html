Thoughts on English learning

@language

English learning guide

Think in more explicit way, having more contents, visualize the world, objects, action, more explicit expression. image what is in the room, what are they doing, how do they look, what's the relation between them, how to describe their looks, wearing, smell, so on.

Make what you think in brain more specific and tangible. Assemble each piece of content put in a logical fashion. Chinese is in packed and condensed form compared with English. It is not rare that there is word consisted with over 10 letters in English. For the same amount of time of pronunciation it can represent actually 4 words in Chinese. English sentences needs be more dispatched to parts. and use more "small" words to link the different concepts.So it is very critical in English to specific what is the host, what is the object and what is the action in between. How to compose a English sentence as native do? Using more noun phrase plays the trick. In one sentence, there is only one predicate verb which indicate the main logic of the sentence. So to link motion concepts, the way is pretty trivial, converting all other verb phrases to noun phase in which way they can be part of sentence as attribute, complement, adverbial clause, etc...

Why my English is not improved, because I didn't have my thoughts in brain explicitly expressed.

When　one develop their own native core code, analogous to computer's binary code, it is built primary in the infant age. The kids observe the world, develop the their understanding of what's going on in the world, and attach these concepts to their "language". Thus it is same as watch the cartoon or movies during their time of infant. What I suggest to refresh this process, because we need learn a second language and it needs provide a second chance for learner to go though this process again. In this way, it is not fist language to second language type of transition, instead, the second language will be in the same position of the first language which directly link the native code of learner's brain.
